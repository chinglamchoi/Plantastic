    FAQ

    What is the list of all collection points in Hong Kong?

    • The list of all collection points in indexed in the Search page. You can access it by clicking the "search" icon.

    What kind of food can I submit to the food collection points?

    • Food Collection Points are exclusively for collecting Packaged Snacks, due to hygiene concerns.
    • Leftover Foods (post-consumer) can be donated via hotels/housing estates that partner with us. To become a partner, please fill out the "Become a Partner" form.

    Where would my donated foods go?

    • Leftover Foods (post-consumer) donations will be collected from hotels/housing estates that we partner with and be delivered to NGOs/companies that transform such foods into plant fertilizers/animal feed.
    • Packaged Snacks donations will be collected from the Food Collection Sites (Food Depositories) and upon successful completion of food safety tests, will be distributed to the undernourished and in need of foods.

    What is a Food Collection Site/Food Depository?

    • Food Collection Sites/Food Depositories are physical, waste-bin-sized comtainers for Packaged Snacks donations. We hope to set them up all over the world, geocoding them such that users may be able to locate nearby Food Collection Sites using our Site's Geolocation Services.

    Food Waste Facts

    Food wastage is leading contributor to Poverty and Environmental issues globally.


    According to a 2016 research by the World Hunger Education Services, 1 in 9 people suffer from chronic undernourishment, due to the lack of food. In spite of this, researches from the Food and Agriculture Organisation of the United Nations, show that 1/3 of all our food produce is thrown away, despite a significant portion being edible excess foods. A mere quarter of all wasted foods would be able to feed all the 795 million undernourished from around the world. The wealth disparity and disregard to food wastage constitutes unequal distribution of food.


    The Food Collector tackles world hunger issues, by providing users with a convenient platform to donate Packaged Snacks. By disposing of clean, unwanted packaged foods to our Food Collection Sites (Food Depositories), users allow us to redistribute such foods to the needy (the undernourished).


    On the other hand, another dire consequence of Food Wastage is Environmental Harm. Citing the the Food and Agriculture Organisation of the United Nations' 2013 research on Food Wastage Footprint, Food Wastage is the third top carbon dioxide emitter, emitting about 3.3 gigatonnes of CO2. Apart from serious carbon dioxide emission levels, overwhelming amounts of food waste is also the largest component of landfills, which are the second-largest human-related source of methane emission. By regulating the amount of food waste, we directly decrease emission of greenhouse gasses, which in turn eliminates a leading cause of Global Warming.


    Through partnerships with major housing estates, hotels, NGOs and food-processing companies, we offer weekly truck services to deliver Leftover Foods from estates/hotels to the NGOs/companies. Instead of ending up in landfills, such foods can now be transformed into useful products, such as plant fertilizers and animal feed. This is a direct, environmentally-friendly way of recycling post-consumer foods and eradicate the environmental harm caused, if the foods were to go to waste.


    The Food Collector is a highly effective solution to both Environmental and Poverty dilemmas caused by Food Wastage. With the Food Collector, everyone, is now empowered to help eradicate the food wastage problem.
